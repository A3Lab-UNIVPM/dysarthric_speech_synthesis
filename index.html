Improving Dysarthric Speech Classification
with Generative AI-based Data Augmentation

Davide Lillini1, Teresa Basili1, Lucia Migliorelli2, Carlo Aironi1, Leonardo Gabrielli1, Stefano Squartini1

1Department of Information Engineering, Università Politecnica delle Marche, Ancona, Italy

Abstract

Dysarthria is a neurological speech disorder characterized by impaired motor control of speech production, resulting in reduced intelligibility. It commonly arises in the context of neurodegenerative conditions, including amyotrophic lateral sclerosis and cerebral palsy. The development of deep learning systems for dysarthria assessment is hindered by the scarcity of annotated data, which restricts the ability of models to generalize to unseen speakers. This study investigates the effectiveness of diffusion-based generative data augmentation for improving automatic speech intelligibility classification from dysarthric speech. A Grad-TTS model is trained on dysarthric speakers from the TORGO dataset and used to synthesise the vocabulary of the UA-Speech dataset, producing speaker-conditioned samples that combine pathological acoustic traits with increased lexical diversity. The impact of synthetic augmentation is evaluated using ResNet-50 and ResNet-152-based classifiers, in both binary and four-class intelligibility classification tasks. The inclusion of synthetic data consistently improves performance over real-data-only training, achieving up to 84% accuracy in binary classification and a weighted-average F1 score of 50% in the four-class task, evaluated on speakers not observed during training.
